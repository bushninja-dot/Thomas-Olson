Summing Forces: Logarithmic Derivatives and Polynomial Terms

In the Coulomb analogy, each root \( \lambda_i \) acts as a "charge," and the pairwise force on a test point \( z \) (not a root) from all roots is the sum \( \sum_i \frac{1}{z - \lambda_i} \). This total "field" relates directly to the polynomial and its first derivative—no extra factors needed beyond the inherent scaling from the degree.

Key Relation: Logarithmic Derivative

For \( P(z) = \prod_i (z - \lambda_i) \) (monic quartic), the logarithmic derivative gives the exact sum of forces:

$$ \frac{P'(z)}{P(z)} = \sum_i \frac{1}{z - \lambda_i} $$

This is the total force from all roots at \( z \). At equilibrium for root \( \lambda_k \), the self-force singularity is excluded, and it balances the confining potential: \( \sum_{i \neq k} \frac{1}{\lambda_k - \lambda_i} = V'(\lambda_k) \) (e.g., \( V' = \lambda_k \) for harmonic).

No ESP coefficients as prefactors here—the sum is direct. However, expanding \( P'(z)/P(z) \) via partial fractions or series involves ESP via Newton identities (e.g., residues tie to \( e_k \)).

Incorporating Higher Derivatives: Full "Force Landscape"

To "add up forces in the derivatives," consider the full electrostatic analogy in the complex plane: the potential is \( \log |P(z)| = \sum_i \log |z - \lambda_i| \), field \( \nabla \log |P| = \operatorname{Re}(P'/P) \) (real part for magnitude force).

Higher derivatives capture multi-body effects:

$$ \frac{P''(z)}{P(z)} - \left( \frac{P'(z)}{P(z)} \right)^2 = - \sum_{i \neq j} \frac{1}{(z - \lambda_i)(z - \lambda_j)} $$

This is the trace of the Hessian (second derivatives), summing pairwise "stiffnesses" \( \partial^2 / \partial z^2 \) of each log term. For quartic, explicit:

$$ P(z) = z^4 - e_1 z^3 + e_2 z^2 - e_3 z + e_4, $$ $$ P'(z) = 4 z^3 - 3 e_1 z^2 + 2 e_2 z - e_3, $$ $$ P''(z) = 12 z^2 - 6 e_1 z + 2 e_2. $$

Notice the factors (4,3,2,1 for P'; 12=4·3,6=3·2,2=2·1 for P'')—these are binomial/DSP coefficients from differentiating the power-sum relations, but not ESP directly as prefactors for the force sum. To express the total field in ESP, use the generating function or recurrence, but the sum \( P'/P \) already "includes" them implicitly.

Equilibrium Condition with Factors

At a root \( \lambda_k \), the force balance is limit as \( z \to \lambda_k \) of \( P'(z)/P(z) - 1/(z - \lambda_k) = \sum_{i \neq k} 1/(\lambda_k - \lambda_i) \), and by L'Hôpital, \( P''(\lambda_k)/P'(\lambda_k) = \) that sum. So higher derivatives enter the balance, with the degree factors (e.g., leading 4 in P') scaling the "strength."

If you mean weighting by ESP (e.g., force ~ e_k / dist^k for multipole), that's an approximation for far-field, but for exact sum, no—use the log derivative directly.

Example: Symmetric Quartic

Roots \( \pm 2, \pm 1 \) (\( e_1 = 0, e_2 = 6, e_3 = 0, e_4 = 4 \)). At z=0 (center):

$$ P(0) = 4, \quad P'(0) = -e_3 = 0, \quad \frac{P'(0)}{P(0)} = 0 = \frac{1}{0-(-2)} + \frac{1}{0-2} + \frac{1}{0-(-1)} + \frac{1}{0-1} = \frac{1}{2} - \frac{1}{2} + 1 - 1 = 0 $$

Balances to zero, as expected by symmetry. For P'': P''(0) = 2 e_2 = 12, and the pairwise Hessian sum matches after factors.
